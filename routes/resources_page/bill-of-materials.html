Bill of Materials

All the parts you need and where to find them.

There are two official stores:

If you are in the US, you can use the Donkeystore. The intention of the Donkey Store is to make it easier and less expensive to build the Donkey Car. The Donkey Store is run by the original founders of donkey car and profits are used to fund development of the donkey cars.

It is also worth noting the design of the parts out of the Donkey store is slightly improved over the standard build as it uses better parts that are only available in large quantities or are harder to get. The Donkey Store builds are open source like all others.

If you are in Asia, the DIYRobocars community in Hong Kong also sells car kits at Robocar Store. They are long term Donkey community members and use proceeds to support the R&D efforts of this project. It is worth noting they can also sell to Europe and the US but it is likely less cost effective.

Parts List:

Adafruit PCA9685 (controller)

In-order to make the motors spin on the car, a very specific pulse is required to tell the motors exactly what to do.  This method is called Pulse-Width Modulation (PWM) and uses small variations in voltage to affect the speed or signal being sent to the motors.  This controller (PCA9685) enables us to send these pulses to the motor.

Raspberry Pi Model 3 (RPi)

The Raspberry Pi is a credit-sized computer that runs Debain Linux.  This will do all the processing of images, sending signals to the control board and run the web-portal for controlling the car.

Raspberry Pi Camera Module V2 - 8.0 MP (Camera)

This is a specialized camera that connects to the RPi’s dedicated CSI port giving direct access to the RPi’s specialized VideoCoreIV GPU.

32 GB Micro SD Card (Storage)

Has the RPi’s Operating System on it, along with any images taken.

USB Battery Bank

This provides power to the Raspberry Pi via a micro USB cable..

Jumper wires x 4

For connecting the RPi to the controller

M2x6 screws (4)

For holding the camera to the roll cage.

M2.5x12 screws (8)

For holding the RPi and controller in place on the roll cage.

M2.5 nuts (8)

For holding the RPi and controller in place on the roll cage.

M2.5 washers (8)

For holding the RPi and controller in place on the roll cage.

3D Printed roll cage and base plate (Roll Cage / Top)

Includes holes for holding all the components in exactly the right spot. You will need to 3D print this before you begin. Link above is to files. You can also purchase from the donkey store.
