1. Form a team

With a team of 3, build a car within the allowed max budget of $500. Like industry giants, they run ML/CV software, from TensorFlow and OpenCV to ROS and Keras.

To keep costs down, they split their computational tasks between Raspberry Pi computers onboard for real-time processing and cloud processing for offline training.

2. Hot laps at school. Compete at hack/race days.

Build the track at school. All you need is some tape. We'll provide the design and dimensions.

Get your car to run as fast as possible.

Then join everyone once a month for hack and race days. You'll have the opportunity to hack, compete, and show and tell. Most importantly, build lasting friendships within the community.

3. Climb the rankings

Every team’s best time is recorded and sorted through a leaderboard. Get rewarded for fast times, creativity, and team work.

Make and race pro-level autonomous cars on a budget.

Just because these cars are small and inexpensive doesn’t mean that you can’t run real autonomous car software on them.

These robocars transmit the data from their on-board sensors (cameras, sonar, lidar, radar, GPS or whatever else you have) via Wifi to a laptop that runs pro-grade AI and robotics software. Some of that includes TensorFlow, ROS and the rest of the Udacity Self-Driving Car toolchain.
